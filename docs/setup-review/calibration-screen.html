Why "Try again" is always shown: The clinician — not the patient — decides whether to retry. The patient just needs the button available. "Continue anyway" is de-emphasized (gray) on marginal to nudge retry, but doesn't block the clinician from accepting.

Annotations & Design Rationale

  • Level bar prominence: The VU meter bar is deliberately oversized (36px tall) so it is legible in a video call screen share at reduced resolution. The clinician is the primary reader of this bar, not the patient.
  • "Your clinician is watching": This line serves two audiences simultaneously — it reassures the patient they are not alone in judging their performance, and it cues the clinician to actively monitor the meter rather than passively observing.
  • Countdown over progress bar: A large numeric countdown (5…4…3…2…1) replaces a generic progress bar because elderly users find numbers more immediately understandable than abstract fill animations. The bar is secondary information.
  • Two result variants: "Good" and "Marginal" map to real acoustic thresholds (signal-to-noise). The marginal state does not block progress — the clinician may accept it if a retry is impractical. An outright failure state (no signal detected) would show a separate error card not shown here.
  • Large tap targets: All buttons are full-width or half-width with ≥48px touch height, suitable for patients with reduced fine motor control.
